DISCUSSION: CHART CHOICES & FORMATTING
----------------------------------------

1. TIME SERIES LINE CHARTS
   - Daily line chart: Shows raw fluctuations (1,430 points). No data labels (too cluttered).
     Formatting: thin blue line, rotated x-labels, grid, legend.
   - Monthly line chart: 48 points, reveals seasonality. No labels (would overlap).
     Formatting: red markers, 90° rotated labels, dashed grid.
   - Quarterly line chart: 16 points, with value labels (best practice: labels only when n <= 20).
     Formatting: green dashed line, square markers, bold dollar labels offset above points.

2. BAR CHART (CATEGORY COMPARISON)
   - Why: Best for comparing discrete categories and showing rank.
   - Sorting: Descending order (largest first).
   - Labels: Dollar amounts above bars (3% offset).
   - Y-axis: Formatted as $1,000,000 with custom ticker.
   - Grid: Only horizontal, dashed, light grey.
   - Spines: Top and right removed for clean look.

3. PIE CHART (SHARE OF SALES)
   - Why: Shows part-to-whole relationship (percentages).
   - Inside labels: Percent + dollar amount (e.g., '37.5% ($4,744,558)').
   - Exploded slice: Largest category (Technology) pulled out slightly.
   - Colours: Matched bar chart for consistency.
   - Caution: Pie charts work best with <=5 categories (here 3 is ideal).

4. GENERAL FORMATTING CHOICES (ALL CHARTS)
   - Resolution: 150 dpi PNG, bbox_inches='tight'.
   - Titles: Bold, size 14, descriptive.
   - Axis labels: Include units ($ or USD).
   - Legends: Placed upper left where needed.
   - Layout: tight_layout() prevents clipping.

5. DATA LABELS BEST PRACTICE
   - Applied only when number of points <= 20 (quarterly chart).
   - Avoided on monthly (48 points) and daily (1,430 points) to prevent clutter.

6. INSIGHTS FROM THE DATA
   - Technology leads with 37.5% of total sales, followed by Furniture (32.5%) and Office Supplies (30.0%).
   - Seasonal pattern: Q4 (October-December) consistently shows highest sales.
   - The distribution is fairly balanced across the three main categories.
